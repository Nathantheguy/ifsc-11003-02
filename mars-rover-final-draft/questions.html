Frequently Asked Questions

Curiosity:

What is the Curiosity rover and when did it land on Mars?

Curiosity is a car-sized robotic rover designed to explore Gale Crater on Mars. It landed on August 6, 2012, as part of NASA’s Mars Science Laboratory (MSL) mission.

What is the main mission of the Curiosity rover?

Curiosity’s primary mission is to determine whether Mars ever had environmental conditions suitable for microbial life. It studies the planet’s climate, geology, and the role of water in shaping its surface.

How does Curiosity study the rocks and soil on Mars?

Curiosity uses a suite of instruments—including lasers, cameras, spectrometers, and a sample-analysis lab—to drill into rocks, scoop up soil, and analyze their chemical and mineral composition.

How does Curiosity navigate across the Martian surface?

The rover relies on onboard hazard-avoidance cameras, stereo imaging, and autonomous navigation software that helps it plan safe driving routes across rough terrain.

What has Curiosity discovered about past water on Mars?

Curiosity has found strong evidence that Gale Crater once hosted long-lasting lakes and streams. It has detected clays, sediments, and chemical signatures that indicate Mars had habitable conditions billions of years ago.

How is the Curiosity rover powered?

Curiosity uses a radioisotope thermoelectric generator (RTG), which converts heat from the natural decay of plutonium-238 into electricity.

How long was Curiosity originally supposed to operate on Mars?

Curiosity was designed for a two-Earth-year mission, but it has exceeded that by many years and continues to operate and send back science data.

Perseverance:

What is the purpose of the Perseverance rover mission?

Perseverance’s mission focuses on searching for signs of ancient microbial life, studying Mars’ geology, collecting rock cores for future return to Earth, and testing new technologies for future human exploration.

When did Perseverance arrive on Mars, and where did it land?

Perseverance landed on February 18, 2021, inside Jezero Crater—an ancient lakebed believed to be one of the most promising places to look for past life.

What scientific instruments does Perseverance carry?

The rover carries a suite of advanced instruments, including ground-penetrating radar, high-resolution cameras, spectrometers, environmental sensors, and systems designed to detect organic compounds and past biosignatures.

How does Perseverance search for signs of ancient life?

It studies layered rock formations, examines chemical compositions, uses ultraviolet and X-ray tools to scan for organics, and takes samples from sites most likely to preserve ancient microbes.

What is the Mars Sample Return campaign, and how does Perseverance support it?

Perseverance drills and caches rock and soil cores in sealed tubes. A future mission—part of a multi-agency campaign—aims to retrieve those samples and return them to Earth for detailed laboratory analysis.

What is the role of the Ingenuity helicopter?

Ingenuity is a small technology-demonstration helicopter that became the first aircraft to achieve powered flight on another planet. It helps scout terrain and test flight operations in Mars’ thin atmosphere.

How does Perseverance communicate with Earth?

The rover communicates through NASA’s Deep Space Network using relays via Mars-orbiting spacecraft, which transmit data and commands between Earth and the rover more efficiently than direct communication alone.
